2/25/2015: Singularity Today, Is the end nigh?

Well it looks like Ray Kurzweil was right. It seems just like yesterday when we were first hearing of his radical predicitons in his self-authored book The Singularity is Near. But here we are today 10 years later and wake up to find out that indeed it has happened. Two reasearch Universities in Fantastic Land have indeed created a machine that can redesign itself in the event that it needs to as well as program other machines better than itself. This is great news for the advancement of technology. Soon we'll be able to send unmanned missions to space and colonize planets or the machine might turn rogue. To be completely honest the only thing I fear is that my programming job might be in jeapordy: who would want to hire a human when a computer can do it? Here's hoping that my career stays relevant until I retire. Regardles, what a time to be alive!

9/19/2015: Quantum Processors in your Pocket

This year is shaping up to be the year in terms of computational advancements. Earlier we achieved the singularity just as Ray Kurzweil had predicted 10 years earlier and today we find out that F-Wave (not to be confused with D-Wave) has released a new Quantum Computing processor codenamed four-two. The news comes from what seems to the be the hotbed of technology: Fantastic Land. A team of researchers there collaborated with F-Wave to create a new manufacturing process that enables the mass production of Quantum Computing processors for mobile phones. This is great news in terms of crazy fast computation in such a small package. In the not too distant future we can all just use our mobile phones to find if N = NP, all of the prime numbers ever - which would break encryption: yikes! - as well as find out if we are indeed just living in a computer simulation!...or the sentient beings will make themselves super powerful and wipe out all life as we know it. Hopefully that last one doesn't come to fruition any time soon.
